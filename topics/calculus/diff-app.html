Applications of Differential Calculus

Use derivatives to find peaks, valleys, rates of change, and optimize real-world problems.

Optimization

Calculus tells us exactly where a function reaches its highest or lowest points. At these peaks and valleys, the slope of the curve is perfectly flat.

$$ f'(x) = 0 $$
To find the maximum or minimum, take the derivative, set it to zero, and solve for $x$.
ConceptCritical Points

Points where $ f'(x) = 0 $ or is undefined.

TestSecond Derivative Test

Helps you determine if a point is a max or min.

If $ f''(x) > 0 $, it's a MINIMUM (concave up).
If $ f''(x) < 0 $, it's a MAXIMUM (concave down).

Find the points of local maxima and minima for $ f(x) = x^3 - 3x $
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: Max at $x=-1$, Min at $x=1$
  • Option B: Max at $x=1$, Min at $x=-1$
  • Option C: Max at $x=0$, Min at $x=3$
  • Option D: No local extrema
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Find critical points: $ f'(x) = 3x^2 - 3 = 0 \implies x^2 = 1 \implies x = \pm 1 $. Second derivative is $ f''(x) = 6x $. Since $ f''(-1) = -6 < 0 $ (Maximum) and $ f''(1) = 6 > 0 $ (Minimum).
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A (Max at $x=-1$, Min at $x=1$)

A particle moves along a line according to $ s(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t $. Find its acceleration at $ t = 4 $ seconds.
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 12 \text{ m/s}^2 $
  • Option B: $ 6 \text{ m/s}^2 $
  • Option C: $ 24 \text{ m/s}^2 $
  • Option D: $ 0 \text{ m/s}^2 $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Velocity $ v(t) = s'(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9 $. Acceleration $ a(t) = v'(t) = 6t - 12 $. At $t = 4$, $ a(4) = 6(4) - 12 = 24 - 12 = 12 \text{ m/s}^2 $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ 12 \text{ m/s}^2 $)

According to Rolle's Theorem, if $f(a) = f(b)$ for a continuous and differentiable function, what must exist in $(a, b)$?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: A point where $ f'(c) = 0 $
  • Option B: A point where $ f(c) = 0 $
  • Option C: A point where $ f'(c) > 0 $
  • Option D: No critical points exist
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Rolle's Theorem states that under these conditions, there must exist at least one point $c$ in open interval $(a, b)$ such that the derivative is zero: $ f'(c) = 0 $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A (A point where $ f'(c) = 0 $)
