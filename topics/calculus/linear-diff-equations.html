Linear Differential Equations

When an equation contains an unknown function AND its derivatives. Learn how to solve using Integrating Factors.

Equations of Change

A Differential Equation (DE) represents a relationship between a changing quantity and its rate of change. Solving a DE doesn't give you a number; it gives you a FUNCTION.

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x) $$
The standard form of a First-Order Linear Differential Equation.
ToolIntegrating Factor (IF)

A magical multiplier that makes the left side of the equation a perfect derivative.

$$ \text{IF} = e^{\int P(x) dx} $$
SolutionGeneral Solution

Once you multiply everything by the IF, integrate both sides to solve for y.

$$ y \cdot \text{IF} = \int Q(x) \cdot \text{IF} \, dx + C $$
