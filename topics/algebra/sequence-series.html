Sequences and Series

Understand patterns of numbers and how to calculate their sums using AP and GP formulas.

Lists vs Sums

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers (e.g., 2, 4, 6, 8...). A series is the sum of those numbers (e.g., 2 + 4 + 6 + 8...).

APArithmetic Progression

Adds a constant difference ($d$) every step.

$$ n^{\text{th}} \text{ term:} \quad a_n = a + (n-1)d $$
Ex: 3, 7, 11, 15... ($d=4$)
Sum of APArithmetic Series

The sum of the first $n$ terms of an AP.

$$ S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n-1)d] $$
GPGeometric Progression

Multiplies by a constant ratio ($r$) every step.

$$ n^{\text{th}} \text{ term:} \quad a_n = a \cdot r^{n-1} $$
Ex: 2, 6, 18, 54... ($r=3$)
Sum of GPGeometric Series

The sum of an infinite GP converges if $|r| < 1$.

$$ S_\infty = \frac{a}{1 - r} $$

Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 16 $
  • Option B: $ 12 $
  • Option C: $ 15 $
  • Option D: The series does not converge
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
First term $a = 8$, common ratio $r = 1/2$. Since $|r| < 1$, the sum is $ S_\infty = \frac{a}{1 - r} = \frac{8}{1 - 0.5} = 16 $.

Final Answer: Option A ($ 16 $)

If the 3rd term of a GP is 12 and the 6th term is 96, find the common ratio $r$.
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 2 $
  • Option B: $ 3 $
  • Option C: $ 4 $
  • Option D: $ 1.5 $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
We know $ a_3 = ar^2 = 12 $ and $ a_6 = ar^5 = 96 $. Divide them: $ \frac{ar^5}{ar^2} = \frac{96}{12} \implies r^3 = 8 \implies r = 2 $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ 2 $)

In a geometric progression, if the common ratio $|r| \ge 1$, what can be said about the infinite sum?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: It is finite
  • Option B: It diverges (is infinite)
  • Option C: It is zero
  • Option D: It equals the first term
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
An infinite geometric series converges to a finite value if and only if the absolute value of the common ratio is strictly less than 1 ($|r| < 1$). Otherwise, it diverges.
