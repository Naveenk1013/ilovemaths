Equations

Balancing the scales. Learn how to solve linear and quadratic equations for unknown variables.

Finding the Unknown

An equation says that two things are equal. Solving an equation means finding the value of the variable that makes the statement true.

$$ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$
The Quadratic Formula. It will solve ANY quadratic equation $ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 $.
Degree 1Linear Equations

Equations with $x^1$. Isolate $x$ on one side.

$$ ax + b = 0 $$
Ex: $2x - 6 = 0 \implies x = 3$
Degree 2Quadratic Equations

Equations with $x^2$. They usually have 2 solutions.

$$ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 $$
CheckThe Discriminant ($\Delta$)

Tells you how many real roots a quadratic has.

$$ \Delta = b^2 - 4ac $$
If $\Delta > 0$, 2 roots. If $\Delta < 0$, no real roots.

  • Option C: No real roots (Complex roots)

Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
A negative discriminant means you are taking the square root of a negative number, which results in imaginary/complex roots.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option C perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
