Complex Numbers

What happens when you take the square root of a negative number? Enter the imaginary unit $i$.

The Imaginary Unit $i$

For centuries, $\sqrt{-1}$ was considered impossible. By defining $i = \sqrt{-1}$, we unlock a whole new 2D plane of mathematics called Complex Numbers ($\mathbb{C}$).

$$ z = a + ib $$
Every complex number has a Real part ($a$) and an Imaginary part ($b$).
RulePowers of $i$

The powers of $i$ cycle every 4 steps.

$$ i^1 = i $$
$$ i^2 = -1 $$
$$ i^3 = -i $$
$$ i^4 = 1 $$
ConjugateComplex Conjugate ($\bar{z}$)

Flip the sign of the imaginary part.

$$ \overline{a + ib} = a - ib $$
Ex: $\overline{3 + 4i} = 3 - 4i$
MagnitudeModulus ($|z|$)

The distance from the origin on the Argand plane.

$$ |a + ib| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} $$
Ex: $|3 + 4i| = \sqrt{9+16} = 5$

Find the conjugate of $ (2 + 3i)(1 - i) $
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 5 - i $
  • Option B: $ 5 + i $
  • Option C: $ 1 - 5i $
  • Option D: $ 1 + 5i $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Expand first: $ (2 + 3i)(1 - i) = 2 - 2i + 3i - 3i^2 = 5 + i $. The conjugate of $5+i$ is $5-i$.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ 5 - i $)

What is the value of $ i^{402} $?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 1 $
  • Option B: $ -1 $
  • Option C: $ i $
  • Option D: $ -i $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Since $i^4 = 1$, we divide the power 402 by 4: $ 402 = 4 \times 100 + 2 $. Thus, $ i^{402} = (i^4)^{100} \cdot i^2 = 1 \cdot (-1) = -1 $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option B perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option B ($ -1 $)

Evaluate the modulus of $ \frac{3 + 4i}{1 - 2i} $
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ \sqrt{5} $
  • Option B: $ 5 $
  • Option C: $ \sqrt{2} $
  • Option D: $ 1 $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Modulus of a quotient is the quotient of their moduli: $ |3+4i| = \sqrt{3^2+4^2}=5 $, and $ |1-2i| = \sqrt{1^2+(-2)^2}=\sqrt{5} $. Thus, the modulus is $ 5/\sqrt{5} = \sqrt{5} $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ \sqrt{5} $)

By De Moivre's Theorem, what is $ (\cos \theta + i\sin \theta)^n $ equal to?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta) $
  • Option B: $ n(\cos \theta + i\sin \theta) $
  • Option C: $ \cos^n \theta + i\sin^n \theta $
  • Option D: $ \cos(n\theta) - i\sin(n\theta) $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
De Moivre's Theorem states that raising a complex number in polar form to the power $n$ multiplies the argument by $n$: $ (\cos \theta + i\sin \theta)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta) $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta) $)
