Binary Operations

Discover how custom operators combine two elements of a set to produce a third element.

Combining Two Elements

A binary operation (often denoted by $*$ or $\circ$) is a rule that takes two inputs from a set and produces one output that belongs to the same set.

$$ *: A \times A \to A $$
Take two elements from $A$, apply $*$, and the result must also be in $A$ (this is called Closure).
PropertyClosure

If you operate on two items in the set, the answer doesn't "escape" the set.

$$ a * b \in A \text{ for all } a,b \in A $$
PropertyCommutative

The order of operands doesn't change the outcome.

$$ a * b = b * a $$
IdentityIdentity Element ($e$)

An element that leaves other elements unchanged.

$$ a * e = a \text{ and } e * a = a $$
InverseInverse Element

An element that combines with $a$ to yield the identity $e$.

$$ a * a^{-1} = e $$
