Inequalities

When things aren't exactly equal. Learn the golden rule of inequalities and interval notation.

More or Less?

An inequality compares two values, showing if one is less than, greater than, or simply not equal to another.

$$ -2x < 6 \implies x > -3 $$
The Golden Rule: Whenever you multiply or divide by a NEGATIVE number, you MUST flip the inequality sign!
StrictLess Than / Greater Than

Does not include the boundary number.

$$ x < 5 $$ or $$ y > 10 $$
Number line: Use an OPEN circle $ \circ $
InclusiveOr Equal To

Includes the boundary number.

$$ x \le 5 $$ or $$ y \ge 10 $$
Number line: Use a CLOSED circle $ \bullet $
NotationIntervals

Writing inequalities as sets of numbers.

$$ x \ge 3 \implies [3, \infty) $$
Parentheses `()` for strict. Brackets `[]` for inclusive.

What is the solution set for the absolute value inequality $ |2x - 3| \le 5 $?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ [-1, 4] $
  • Option B: $ (-\infty, -1] \cup [4, \infty) $
  • Option C: $ [1, 4] $
  • Option D: $ [-4, 1] $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Rewrite as a compound inequality: $ -5 \le 2x - 3 \le 5 $. Add 3 to all parts: $ -2 \le 2x \le 8 $. Divide by 2: $ -1 \le x \le 4 $, which is the interval $ [-1, 4] $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ [-1, 4] $)

Factor the quadratic expression: $ (x - 5)(x + 2) > 0 $. The critical points are -2 and 5. Testing intervals shows the product is positive when $x < -2$ or $x > 5$.

Step 1
Analyze the given options:

  • Option B: $ ac > bc $
  • Option C: $ a/c < b/c $
  • Option D: $ ac = bc $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number reverses the direction of the inequality sign. Thus, $ ac > bc $.

Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option B perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option B ($ ac > bc $)

Find the domain of $ f(x) = \sqrt{x^2 - 9} $
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ x \ge 3 $
  • Option B: $ (-\infty, -3] \cup [3, \infty) $
  • Option C: $ [-3, 3] $
  • Option D: $ x > 3 $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
The expression inside the square root must be non-negative: $ x^2 - 9 \ge 0 \implies (x-3)(x+3) \ge 0 $. This holds true for $x \le -3$ or $x \ge 3$.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option B perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option B ($ (-\infty, -3] \cup [3, \infty) $)
