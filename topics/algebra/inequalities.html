Inequalities

When things aren't exactly equal. Learn the golden rule of inequalities and interval notation.

More or Less?

An inequality compares two values, showing if one is less than, greater than, or simply not equal to another.

$$ -2x < 6 \implies x > -3 $$
The Golden Rule: Whenever you multiply or divide by a NEGATIVE number, you MUST flip the inequality sign!
StrictLess Than / Greater Than

Does not include the boundary number.

$$ x < 5 $$ or $$ y > 10 $$
Number line: Use an OPEN circle $ \circ $
InclusiveOr Equal To

Includes the boundary number.

$$ x \le 5 $$ or $$ y \ge 10 $$
Number line: Use a CLOSED circle $ \bullet $
NotationIntervals

Writing inequalities as sets of numbers.

$$ x \ge 3 \implies [3, \infty) $$
Parentheses `()` for strict. Brackets `[]` for inclusive.

Solve the inequality for $x$:
$\frac{x - 3}{x + 2} \ge 0$
Step 1
$\text{Critical points: } x = 3 \quad \text{and} \quad x = -2$
Identify critical values where numerator is 0 or denominator is undefined.
Step 2
$\text{Intervals: } (-\infty, -2), \, (-2, 3], \, [3, \infty)$
Map out the test intervals on a number line.
Step 3
$\text{Test } x=4 \implies (+)/(+) \ge 0 \quad (\text{True})$
Test a point in each interval to find where the rational function holds true.
Step 4
$x \in (-\infty, -2) \cup [3, \infty)$
Exclude critical point $x = -2$ since it makes the denominator zero.
Final Answer: $x \in (-\infty, -2) \cup [3, \infty)$
