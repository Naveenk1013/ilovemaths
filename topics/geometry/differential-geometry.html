Differential Geometry

The math of General Relativity. Measure curves and surfaces in space using calculus.

Bending Space

Differential geometry applies calculus to study smooth shapes and spaces. It describes how much curves bend and how much surfaces twist.

$$ \kappa = \left| \frac{d\mathbf{T}}{ds} \right| $$
Curvature ($\kappa$). Measures how sharply a curve bends as you move along it.
ConceptTorsion ($\tau$)

Measures how sharply a curve twists out of its plane (like a spring).

ConceptGaussian Curvature

An intrinsic property of a surface. A cylinder is "flat" ($K=0$), but a sphere is curved ($K>0$).
