Vectors

Quantities with both magnitude (size) and direction. The arrows that define physics and 3D space.

Arrows in Space

Unlike a scalar (like temperature) which only has an amount, a vector has an amount AND a direction (like wind blowing 10mph North).

$$ \vec{v} = x\hat{i} + y\hat{j} + z\hat{k} $$
Component form. $i, j, k$ point along the $X, Y, Z$ axes.
LengthMagnitude ($|\vec{v}|$)

The physical length of the arrow using Pythagoras.

$$ |\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} $$
Scalar AnsDot Product ($\cdot$)

Measures how much two vectors point in the same direction. Returns a number.

$$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \cos(\theta) $$
Vector AnsCross Product ($\times$)

Produces a new vector perpendicular to BOTH original vectors.

$$ |\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \sin(\theta) $$

Find the dot product $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}$ of vectors:
$\vec{u} = 2\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}, \quad \vec{v} = -1\hat{i} + 4\hat{j}$
Step 1
$\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_x v_x + u_y v_y$
State the algebraic formula for vector dot products.
Step 2
$\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = (2 \cdot -1) + (3 \cdot 4)$
Multiply corresponding dimensions of both vectors together.
Step 3
$\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = -2 + 12 = 10$
Sum the dimensional products to get the scalar dot product value.
Final Answer: $10$
