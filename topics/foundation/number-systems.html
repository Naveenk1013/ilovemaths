Number Systems

Understand the classification of numbers: Natural, Whole, Integers, Rational, Irrational, and Real numbers.

The Hierarchy of Numbers

Mathematics classifies numbers into different sets. Each set is a broader collection that includes the ones before it. Let's build them up from scratch.

$$ \mathbb{N} \subset \mathbb{W} \subset \mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q} \subset \mathbb{R} $$
Every Natural number is a Whole number. Every Whole number is an Integer. Every Integer is Rational. Every Rational is a Real number.
$\mathbb{N}$ Natural Numbers

Counting numbers starting from 1.

$$ \mathbb{N} = \{1, 2, 3, 4, ...\} $$
Analogy: Counting physical objects like apples. You can't have 0 apples when counting.
$\mathbb{W}$ Whole Numbers

Natural numbers + Zero.

$$ \mathbb{W} = \{0, 1, 2, 3, ...\} $$
Analogy: Counting apples, including an empty basket (0).
$\mathbb{Z}$ Integers

Whole numbers + Negative numbers.

$$ \mathbb{Z} = \{..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...\} $$
Analogy: A bank account. Positive is money you have, negative is debt.
$\mathbb{Q}$ Rational Numbers

Any number that can be written as a fraction $\frac{p}{q}$ where $q \neq 0$.

$$ \mathbb{Q} = \left\{ \frac{p}{q} \mid p, q \in \mathbb{Z}, q \neq 0 \right\} $$
$$ 0.5 = \frac{1}{2} $$  |  $$ 5 = \frac{5}{1} $$  |  $$ 0.333... = \frac{1}{3} $$
$\mathbb{Q}'$ Irrational Numbers

Numbers that CANNOT be written as a simple fraction. Their decimals go on forever without repeating.

$$ \pi, \sqrt{2}, e $$
$$ \sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421... $$ (never repeats)
$\mathbb{R}$ Real Numbers

All Rational and Irrational numbers combined.

$$ \mathbb{R} = \mathbb{Q} \cup \mathbb{Q}' $$
Analogy: Every possible point on a continuous number line.
Quick Revision Box
Is 0 a natural number?No, it's a Whole Number.
Is 5 a rational number?Yes, because $5 = \frac{5}{1}$.
Is $ \pi $ exactly $\frac{22}{7}$?No, $\frac{22}{7}$ is just an approximation.
Are decimals rational?Yes, IF they terminate or repeat (e.g. $0.33...$).
Score: 0 / 0
✓ Repeating vs. Non-Repeating
If a decimal repeats (like $$ 0.6666... $$), it is Rational because it can be written as $$ \frac{2}{3} $$. If it never repeats and never ends (like $$ \sqrt{3} $$), it is Irrational.
# The $\sqrt{}$ Trap
Not all square roots are irrational! For example, $$ \sqrt{16} = 4 $$, which is a Natural number, Integer, and Rational number. Only roots of non-perfect squares are irrational.
