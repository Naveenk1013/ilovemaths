Trigonometry

The study of triangles. Understand SOH CAH TOA, the Unit Circle, and core identities.

Right-Angled Triangles

Trigonometry links the angles of a triangle to the lengths of its sides. For a right-angled triangle, we define the three primary ratios based on the angle $ \theta $.

$$ \sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1 $$
The Pythagorean Identity. It holds true for any angle $ \theta $.
SOHSine ($ \sin $)

Opposite over Hypotenuse.

$$ \sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}} $$
CAHCosine ($ \cos $)

Adjacent over Hypotenuse.

$$ \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}} $$
TOATangent ($ \tan $)

Opposite over Adjacent.

$$ \tan(\theta) = \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)} $$
ReciprocalsCosec, Sec, Cot

The flipped versions of the primary ratios.

$$ \csc = 1/\sin $$
$$ \sec = 1/\cos $$
$$ \cot = 1/\tan $$

What is the value of $ \sin^2(35^\circ) + \sin^2(55^\circ) $?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ 1 $
  • Option B: $ 0 $
  • Option C: $ 0.5 $
  • Option D: $ 2 $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Note that $ 55^\circ = 90^\circ - 35^\circ $. Since $ \sin(90^\circ - x) = \cos x $, we have $ \sin^2(55^\circ) = \cos^2(35^\circ) $. By the Pythagorean identity, $ \sin^2(35^\circ) + \cos^2(35^\circ) = 1 $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ 1 $)

Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Since $ \tan \theta = \text{Opposite/Adjacent} = 3/4 $, the hypotenuse is $ \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5 $. Hence, $ \sin \theta = \text{Opposite/Hypotenuse} = 3/5 $.

Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
