Trigonometry

The study of triangles. Understand SOH CAH TOA, the Unit Circle, and core identities.

Right-Angled Triangles

Trigonometry links the angles of a triangle to the lengths of its sides. For a right-angled triangle, we define the three primary ratios based on the angle $ \theta $.

$$ \sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1 $$
The Pythagorean Identity. It holds true for any angle $ \theta $.
SOHSine ($ \sin $)

Opposite over Hypotenuse.

$$ \sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}} $$
CAHCosine ($ \cos $)

Adjacent over Hypotenuse.

$$ \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}} $$
TOATangent ($ \tan $)

Opposite over Adjacent.

$$ \tan(\theta) = \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)} $$
ReciprocalsCosec, Sec, Cot

The flipped versions of the primary ratios.

$$ \csc = 1/\sin $$
$$ \sec = 1/\cos $$
$$ \cot = 1/\tan $$
