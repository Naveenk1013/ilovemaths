Mathematical Logic

Learn how to evaluate arguments using propositions, truth tables, and logical operators.

Propositions

A proposition is a statement that is either definitively True (T) or definitively False (F), but not both.

$$ P \implies Q $$
"If P, then Q." The foundation of mathematical proofs.
ANDConjunction ($ \land $)

True ONLY if BOTH $P$ and $Q$ are True.

$$ P \land Q $$
ORDisjunction ($ \lor $)

True if AT LEAST ONE of $P$ or $Q$ is True.

$$ P \lor Q $$
NOTNegation ($ \neg $)

Flips the truth value.

$$ \neg P $$
IF-THENImplication ($ \implies $)

False ONLY when $P$ is True and $Q$ is False.

$$ P \implies Q $$

What is the truth value of $ P \land (Q \lor \neg P) $ when $P$ is True and $Q$ is False?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: False
  • Option B: True
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Evaluate: $P = T$, $Q = F$. First, $\neg P = F$. Then, $Q \lor \neg P = F \lor F = F$. Finally, $P \land F = T \land F = F$ (False).
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A (False)

What is the negation of De Morgan's Law for $ \neg(P \lor Q) $?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ \neg P \land \neg Q $
  • Option B: $ \neg P \lor \neg Q $
  • Option C: $ P \land Q $
  • Option D: $ \neg P \to \neg Q $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
De Morgan's Law states that the negation of a disjunction is the conjunction of the negations: $ \neg(P \lor Q) \equiv \neg P \land \neg Q $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ \neg P \land \neg Q $)

Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
An implication $ P \to Q $ is conditionally defined as false if and only if the premise ($P$) is true but the conclusion ($Q$) is false.
