Mathematical Logic

Learn how to evaluate arguments using propositions, truth tables, and logical operators.

Propositions

A proposition is a statement that is either definitively True (T) or definitively False (F), but not both.

$$ P \implies Q $$
"If P, then Q." The foundation of mathematical proofs.
ANDConjunction ($ \land $)

True ONLY if BOTH $P$ and $Q$ are True.

$$ P \land Q $$
ORDisjunction ($ \lor $)

True if AT LEAST ONE of $P$ or $Q$ is True.

$$ P \lor Q $$
NOTNegation ($ \neg $)

Flips the truth value.

$$ \neg P $$
IF-THENImplication ($ \implies $)

False ONLY when $P$ is True and $Q$ is False.

$$ P \implies Q $$

Construct a truth value analysis for the logical implication:
$P \to (Q \lor \neg P)$ when $P$ is True ($T$) and $Q$ is False ($F$).
Step 1
$\neg P = \neg T = F$
Evaluate the negation of statement $P$ first.
Step 2
$Q \lor \neg P = F \lor F = F$
Evaluate the disjunction (OR) between $Q$ and $\neg P$.
Step 3
$P \to (Q \lor \neg P) = T \to F = F$
Apply conditional implication: $T \to F$ always evaluates to False ($F$).
Final Answer: The truth value is False ($F$).
