Combinatorics Basics

Learn how to count without actually counting. Discover the fundamental principles of probability and arrangements.

The Art of Counting

Combinatorics is the branch of mathematics dealing with combinations of objects belonging to a finite set. It tells us how many ways an event can happen.

$$ m \times n \text{ ways} $$
Multiplication Principle: If task A can be done in $m$ ways and task B in $n$ ways, doing both takes $m \times n$ ways.
Rule 1Multiplication Principle (AND)

Used when events happen together or in sequence.

$$ \text{Total} = m \times n $$
3 shirts AND 2 pants = $ 3 \times 2 = 6 $ outfits.
Rule 2Addition Principle (OR)

Used when events are mutually exclusive (cannot happen at the same time).

$$ \text{Total} = m + n $$
Take a bus (3 routes) OR a train (2 routes) = $ 3 + 2 = 5 $ ways.
TheoryPigeonhole Principle

If you have more pigeons than pigeonholes, at least one hole must contain more than one pigeon.

If $ n > m $, a hole gets $ \ge 2 $.
