Probability

The math of chance. Learn how to calculate the likelihood of events happening.

Measuring Uncertainty

Probability is a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain) that measures the likelihood of a specific event occurring.

$$ P(A) = \frac{\text{Successful Outcomes}}{\text{Total Possible Outcomes}} $$
The basic formula for Theoretical Probability.
ConceptSample Space ($S$)

The set of all possible outcomes.

Rolling a standard die: $ S = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\} $
RuleComplementary Events

The probability of an event NOT happening.

$$ P(A') = 1 - P(A) $$

Apply the addition rule of probability: $ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) = 0.6 + 0.4 - 0.2 = 0.8 $.

What is the conditional probability formula for $ P(A \mid B) $?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: $ P(A \cap B) / P(B) $
  • Option B: $ P(A \cup B) / P(B) $
  • Option C: $ P(A) \cdot P(B) $
  • Option D: $ P(A \cap B) / P(A) $
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
Conditional probability of $A$ given $B$ is the probability of both occurring divided by the probability of the condition: $ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $ (for $P(B) > 0$).
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ P(A \cap B) / P(B) $)
