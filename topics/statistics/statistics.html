Descriptive Statistics

Summarize large datasets into simple numbers: Mean, Median, Mode, and Standard Deviation.

Measures of Central Tendency

Where is the "middle" of your data? Three different numbers answer this question in different ways.

$$ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} $$
The Mean (Average). Sum everything up and divide by the count.
CenterMedian & Mode

Median is the exact middle value. Mode is the most frequent value.

SpreadVariance ($\sigma^2$)

The average of the squared differences from the Mean.

$$ \sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x - \bar{x})^2}{n} $$
SpreadStandard Deviation ($\sigma$)

The square root of the variance. It tells you how spread out the data is.

$$ \sigma = \sqrt{\text{Variance}} $$
