Discrete Probability Distributions

Model random variables using Binomial and Poisson distributions.

Random Variables

A discrete random variable takes on countable values (like $0, 1, 2...$). A probability distribution assigns a probability to each of these outcomes.

$$ P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$
The Binomial Distribution. Models the probability of $k$ successes in $n$ independent trials.
ModelBinomial Criteria

Requires a fixed number of trials, two outcomes (success/fail), and a constant probability.

ModelPoisson Distribution

Models the number of events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space.

$$ P(X=k) = \frac{\lambda^k e^{-\lambda}}{k!} $$
