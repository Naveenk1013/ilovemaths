Statistical Estimation

Using a small sample to predict the behavior of an entire population.

Samples vs Populations

You rarely have data for an entire population. Instead, you take a sample, calculate statistics, and use those to estimate the true population parameters.

$$ \text{CI} = \bar{x} \pm Z \left( \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \right) $$
Confidence Interval for the Mean. Gives a range of values where the true population mean likely sits.
ConceptPoint Estimate

A single value (like sample mean) used to estimate the population value.

ConceptMargin of Error

The plus/minus amount attached to your point estimate.

To decrease the margin of error in a confidence interval, what should you do?
Step 1
Analyze the given options:
  • Option A: Decrease sample size
  • Option B: Increase sample size
  • Option C: Change the mean
  • Option D: Nothing
Step 2
$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
A larger sample size (n) shrinks the standard error, making your estimate more precise.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option B perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option B (Increase sample size)
Find a 95% Confidence Interval for sample mean $\bar{x} = 50$, standard deviation $\sigma = 10$, and sample size $n = 100$. (Use $z = 1.96$)
Step 1
$\text{Margin of Error } E = z \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$
State the confidence interval error margin formula.
Step 2
$E = 1.96 \cdot \frac{10}{\sqrt{100}} = 1.96 \cdot 1 = 1.96$
Substitute the given values into the formula and solve.
Step 3
$\text{CI} = [50 - 1.96, \, 50 + 1.96] = [48.04, \, 51.96]$
Subtract and add the margin of error from the sample mean.
Final Answer: $[48.04, \, 51.96]$
