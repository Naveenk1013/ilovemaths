Determinants

A single scalar value that tells you if a matrix has an inverse and how it scales geometric areas.

The Scalar Value of a Matrix

Every square matrix has a determinant. It's a special number that reveals deep properties about the matrix, like whether its equations have a unique solution.

$$ |A| = \det \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = ad - bc $$
The 2x2 Determinant: Cross-multiply the main diagonal, subtract the other diagonal.
PropertyInvertibility

If the determinant is zero, the matrix is "singular" and has NO inverse.

$$ |A| \neq 0 \implies \text{Inverse Exists} $$
Method3x3 Expansion

Expand across a row using alternating signs ($+ - +$).

$$ a(ei-fh) - b(di-fg) + c(dh-eg) $$
VisualArea & Volume

The determinant geometrically represents the scaling factor of area (2D) or volume (3D) when the matrix is applied as a transformation.

Example 1 · 2x2 Determinant
00:00
Evaluate the determinant of matrix $A$:
$A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 2 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$
Step 1
$\det(A) = ad - bc$
Use the standard formula for a 2x2 matrix determinant.
Step 2
$\det(A) = (3 \cdot -1) - (4 \cdot 2)$
Substitute diagonal ($3 \cdot -1$) and anti-diagonal ($4 \cdot 2$) values.
Step 3
$\det(A) = -3 - 8 = -11$
Subtract the product values to compute the final determinant.
Final Answer: $\det(A) = -11$
