Matrices

Grids of numbers that power computer graphics, artificial intelligence, and quantum mechanics.

Rows and Columns

A matrix is a rectangular array of numbers arranged in rows (horizontal) and columns (vertical). We define its size as $ \text{Rows} \times \text{Columns} $.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \times \begin{bmatrix} e \\ f \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ae + bf \\ ce + df \end{bmatrix} $$
Matrix Multiplication: "Row by Column". Dive across the row while diving down the column.
BasicsAddition / Subtraction

Matrices must be the EXACT same size. Add or subtract corresponding elements.

$$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} $$
OperationScalar Multiplication

Multiply every single element inside the matrix by a constant number outside.

$$ 2 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} $$
IdentityIdentity Matrix ($I$)

A square matrix with 1s on the main diagonal and 0s everywhere else. $ AI = A $.

$$ I_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$

$\text{Apply Core Principles}$
First, determinant $ \det(A) = 4(2) - 7(1) = 1 $. The inverse is $ \frac{1}{\det(A)} \text{adj}(A) = 1 \cdot \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -7 \\ -1 & 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -7 \\ -1 & 4 \end{pmatrix} $.
Step 3
Cross-reference choices to select the valid deduction. Option A perfectly aligns with the principles analyzed.
Final Answer: Option A ($ \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -7 \\ -1 & 4 \end{pmatrix} $)
